💡 Practical Tips

From Me to You

1

Study real spaces

Visit hotels, restaurants, malls — not as a customer, but as a designer. Analyse everything: the lighting, the flow, the mood.

2

Software is your friend

Get comfortable with AutoCAD, SketchUp, and 3ds Max. These are your brushes as an interior designer — master them and doors open.

3

Keep a design journal

Sketch rough ideas, paste photos, collect fabric swatches. Your physical journal is where your best designs begin, long before the screen.

4

Don't fear your bold ideas

The safest designs are often the most forgettable. Your instinct to go bolder — trust it. That's where your signature style lives.

5

Understand materials deeply

Texture, acoustics, light reflection — the difference between a good designer and a great one is loving the materials, not just the looks.

6

Rest and recharge

Creativity needs space. Don't burn yourself out. Take walks, breathe, look at the sky. Your best ideas will find you when you're calm.
